## Key Implementation Details

### 1. `adjust()` dispatch pattern

The function short-circuits early for `END_OF_MONTH` (which always acts, even on business days) and for already-business-day inputs (no-op for the other four). Then dispatches per convention. This avoids redundant `is_business_day` calls in helpers.

### 2. `MODIFIED_FOLLOWING` month-crossing check

After computing the next BD, compares `.month` of the result vs the original date. If different, falls back to `_last_bd_of_month()`. This correctly handles holiday chains at month-end (e.g. Good Friday + weekend = two consecutive non-BDs).

### 3. `END_OF_MONTH` semantics

`END_OF_MONTH` always returns the last business day of the candidate's month, regardless of whether the input is itself a business day. This is the standard FX tenor roll convention: if a tenor falls on a month-end, it stays at month-end.
